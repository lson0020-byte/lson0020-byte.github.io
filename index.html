A Multi-dimensional Reinterpretation of the Doomsday Clock
The Doomsday Clock is one of the most recognisable scientific symbols in modern history. It was first created in 1947 by the Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists, a group of researchers who had worked on the Manhattan Project. Following the end of World War II, these scientists were deeply concerned about the catastrophic potential of nuclear weapons. They wanted a way to warn the public about how close humanity was to destroying the world. The artist Martyl Langsdorf designed the original visual. She chose a clock because it is a universal tool that everyone understands. In this design, “Midnight” does not represent a new day, but rather the end of human civilization. The closer the minute hand moves to midnight, the more dangerous the global situation becomes. In its first year, the clock was set at seven minutes to midnight, a decision based on the growing tension between global powers at the start of the Cold War.
